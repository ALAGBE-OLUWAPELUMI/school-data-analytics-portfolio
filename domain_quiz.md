An MIS (Management Information System) is the central database software a school uses to store and manage all pupil, staff, and school data. Three common modules within an MIS are Attendance, Behaviour, and Safeguarding.
MyChildAtSchool is a parent portal and mobile application used by parents and carers. It allows them to access real-time details about their child’s education, such as academic reports, attendance, and behaviour logs, as well as enabling them to make online payments for things like school dinners or trips.

Pupil Premium is additional funding provided to state-funded schools in England to help improve the outcomes of disadvantaged children (e.g., those eligible for free school meals or who are looked after).  2025–26 Rate: The rate is £1,075 per eligible secondary-aged pupil (Years 7 to 11).  Tracking it: A school must flag eligible students accurately in their MIS and include them in the School Census so the government calculates funding correctly. Schools must also track the academic progress, attendance, and behaviour of this specific cohort to report on how the funding is closing the attainment gap.
What it is: The School Census is a mandatory statutory data return where state schools submit comprehensive pupil and school-level data to the Department for Education (DfE).  Frequency: It takes place three times a year (Autumn, Spring, and Summer terms).  Usage: It is primarily used to determine the exact amount of government funding a school receives (including Pupil Premium allocations) based on the demographics and numbers of the pupils enrolled.
When a pupil leaves a school, the data assistant must ensure their academic and safeguarding records follow them. The school generates a CTF (Common Transfer File) from their MIS, which is a standardized digital file containing the pupil's core data.This file is uploaded to S2S (School-to-School), a secure government portal, where the pupil's new destination school can download it and ingest the data into their own MIS. If a pupil leaves but their destination is unknown, the school must alert the local authority and log them as CME (Child Missing Education) to ensure the child does not fall through the cracks and remains safe.
